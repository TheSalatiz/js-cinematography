About Me

Filmmaker, Storyteller, Artist

My name is Juan Salazar. I’m a Colombian filmmaker and visual storyteller, but that’s just the surface.

Back home, my first camera was my phone. I didn’t have gear or connections, just the instinct to record moments that felt important.

When I moved to the US to study film and multimedia, everything changed and also, nothing did. I learned the craft, the tools, the language of production. I became a cinematographer, an editor, and a producer. But I’m still chasing the same feeling I had when I was 15, filming with shaky hands, trying to capture something real.

For me, filmmaking is about presence. It’s not just how it looks. It’s how it feels. I work with natural light, clean frames, and honest pacing. Whether I’m shooting a personal short, a live event, or a branded story, my goal is to make the viewer stop for a second and feel seen.

I carry more than just a camera. I carry the responsibility of a dream that doesn’t just belong to me. My work is my way of giving back, of building something solid enough to stand on, and maybe one day, let someone else climb it too.
